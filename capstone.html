Capstone Project

Voices of America: From Colonies to Constitution

Your Mission

Show what you've learned by answering the essential question for each of the four units using evidence from our studies.

Project Overview

You will create either a WeVideo OR a 4-panel poster that demonstrates your understanding of all four units we studied this semester.

Essential Questions You Must Answer:

  • Unit 1: How did geography and economic systems create different societies in colonial America?
  • Unit 2: How did the Triangle Trade create wealth for some while causing suffering for others?
  • Unit 3: Why did different groups have such different perspectives on the American Revolution?
  • Unit 4: How did the founders balance strong government with protection of individual rights?

Choose Your Format

Option A

WeVideo

Length: 3-5 minutes

Requirements:

  • Answer all 4 essential questions
  • Include images, video clips, or visuals
  • Add narration or text explaining your answers
  • Use the Answer-Evidence-Impact framework for each unit
  • Include a title slide and conclusion
Option B

4-Panel Poster

Format: One panel per unit

Requirements:

  • Each panel answers one essential question
  • Include visuals (drawings, images, diagrams)
  • Use the Answer-Evidence-Impact framework for each unit
  • Clear, readable text with good organization
  • Creative and visually engaging design

The Answer-Evidence-Impact Framework

For each unit, you must structure your response using this three-part framework:

1. Answer
Directly answer the essential question in 1-2 sentences. This is your main argument or thesis.
Example: "Geography and climate shaped colonial economies because different regions had different natural resources and growing conditions."
2. Evidence
Provide specific examples from your notes, activities, and readings to support your answer. Use at least 2-3 pieces of evidence.
Example: "New England had rocky soil and cold winters, so they focused on fishing and shipbuilding. The South had fertile soil and warm climate, perfect for growing tobacco and rice on large plantations."
3. Impact
Explain why this matters. What were the consequences or significance of what you described?
Example: "These economic differences created very different ways of life and values. This led to different views on slavery, government, and eventually contributed to tensions that would last for centuries."

What to Include

Make sure your project demonstrates that you understand:

  • Key Vocabulary Use important terms correctly (mercantilism, representative government, checks and balances, etc.)
  • Important People Reference specific individuals we studied (Phillis Wheatley, James Madison, etc.)
  • Significant Events Connect to major events (Constitutional Convention, Middle Passage, etc.)
  • Multiple Perspectives Show understanding that different groups experienced these events differently
  • Cause and Effect Explain how one thing led to another

How You'll Be Graded

Your capstone will be evaluated on:

Content & Understanding

  • All 4 questions answered
  • Accurate information
  • Clear use of A-E-I framework
  • Specific evidence from class

Critical Thinking

  • Explains "why" not just "what"
  • Makes connections between units
  • Shows understanding of impact
  • Multiple perspectives included

Presentation & Creativity

  • Well-organized and clear
  • Engaging visuals
  • Professional appearance
  • Effort and creativity shown

Communication

  • Clear and understandable
  • Proper grammar/spelling
  • Vocabulary used correctly
  • Well-rehearsed (if presenting)

Tips for Success

  • Review all four unit pages on this website before starting
  • Use your notes, handouts, and class activities as evidence
  • Plan your project before you start creating it
  • Make sure you answer ALL parts: Answer, Evidence, AND Impact for each unit
  • Use specific examples - don't just say "people disagreed," say WHO and WHY
  • Check your work for spelling and accuracy
  • Practice your presentation if you're doing a WeVideo or presenting your poster
  • Ask questions if you're unsure about anything!
